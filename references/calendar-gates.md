# Calendar gates, check the PERIOD before the day

**The rule a real pandit applies first:** before judging a weekday / tithi / nakshatra, check whether the *period itself* is blocked. If it is, **no day inside it is good for a new beginning or a big purchase**, postpone past the gate. (Routine, ongoing work continues normally; it's *new starts, launches, registrations, and big buys* that wait.)

**Always run `scripts/panchang.py <date>` first**, these gates can't be eyeballed (they depend on Moon/Sun positions). It reports Adhik Maas, Kharmas, the day's tithi/nakshatra, Rahu Kaal, and Mercury retrograde. If pyephem isn't installed: `pip install ephem`, or verify on drikpanchang.com.

## The gates (most widely observed first)
| Gate | What it is | What to avoid | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adhik Maas** (Mal / Purushottam maas) | An extra lunar month with no solar sankranti; ~every 32-33 months | New undertakings, launches, registrations, big purchases (gold / vehicle / property), housewarming, weddings. *(Puja, japa, charity are encouraged.)* | Compute. **2026: 17 May - 15 Jun.** |
| **Kharmas / Malmas** | Sun in Sagittarius (Dhanurmas) or Pisces (Meenmas) | Auspicious starts, weddings | ~16 Dec-14 Jan & ~15 Mar-13 Apr |
| **Chaturmas** | Vishnu "sleeps": Devshayani → Devuthani Ekadashi | Weddings & many big launches (business is more lenient) | ~Jul-Nov (2026 ≈ 25 Jul - 20 Nov) |
| **Pitru Paksha (Shradh)** | 16 days of ancestor rites | New purchases & launches | ~Sep (Bhadrapada) |
| **Holashtak** | 8 days before Holi | Auspicious work | ~Mar |
| **Eclipse + Sutak** | Solar/lunar eclipse + the hours before it | All new / auspicious work | Compute / panchang |
| **Bhadra (Vishti karana) · Panchak** | Inauspicious karana; Moon in Dhanishta-½ → Revati | Bhadra: starts; Panchak: roofing, southward travel, buying fuel | Within certain tithis/days |

## How to use it in a consult
1. **Run the gate check first.** If a gate is active → the simple answer is **"wait until {gate ends},"** then pick a good day after it.
2. If clear → go to the **day** (weekday, waxing moon, nakshatra, tithi) and **time** (Rahu Kaal) in `muhurta.md`.
3. **Business is more lenient than wedding muhurta**, but **Adhik Maas, eclipses, and Pitru Paksha** are honoured even for business starts and big buys. Postponing a *new start* past these costs little and respects the very tradition the user is consulting.
